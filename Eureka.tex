\section*{The Knif}
%==================================================

Everyone in Labor seems to picture the post-LLM with \emph{nouns}.
Eloundou et al.\ (2024): which \emph{tasks} can GPT-4 do?
Felten et al.\ (2021): which \emph{abilities} does AI replicate?
Autor's expertise agenda (highest respect... I really learnt from this approach) which \emph{knowledge domains} are appearing or disappearing? --> in practice, all nouns? --> I need to verify this

Nouns tell you \textbf{what} is changing.
They do not tell you \textbf{how} people are using it. 

Wittgenstein -- 

The Anthropic Economic Index (Handa et al.\ 2025) reveals that 57\% of
real-world AI usage is augmentation and 43\% is automation.
Both channels are live.  But they don't decompose this at the task level.

\medskip
\noindent I propose: \textbf{the verb is the signal}.

Every O*NET task is a sentence: \textsc{Verb} + object + context.
The noun tells you the domain.  The verb tells you the cognitive mode ---
whether the human is \emph{executing} or \emph{thinking}.

\begin{quote}
\small
``\textbf{Calculate} tax liabilities'' --- execution. AI does this alone.\\
``\textbf{Evaluate} treatment options'' --- judgment. AI assists; human decides.\\
``\textbf{Design} database systems'' --- creation. Human leads; AI drafts.
\end{quote}

This maps onto Bloom's Taxonomy:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{clcl}
\toprule
Level & Bloom & & Channel \\
\midrule
1 & Remember    & \multirow{3}{*}{\textcolor{red}{\textbf{Automation}}} & \multirow{3}{*}{Human dispensable} \\
2 & Understand  & & \\
3 & Apply       & & \\
\midrule
4 & Analyze     & \multirow{3}{*}{\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{Augmentation}}} & \multirow{3}{*}{Human essential} \\
5 & Evaluate    & & \\
6 & Create      & & \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Wittgenstein: ``The meaning of a word is its use in the language.''
The meaning of AI exposure is in the \emph{verbs} people let AI do.

\section*{Construction}
%==================================================

Extract root verb (spaCy) $\to$ Bloom level (${\sim}$300 verbs, ${\sim}$95\% coverage)
$\to$ binary: auto if $\leq 3$, aug if $\geq 4$ $\to$ weight by
Anthropic penetration $\times$ O*NET task importance $\to$ aggregate:
\begin{align}
  \text{AutoIndex}_o &= \frac{\sum_{t} \text{pen}_t \cdot \mathbf{1}[\text{Bloom} \leq 3] \cdot w_t}{\sum_{t} w_t} \\[4pt]
  \text{AugIndex}_o  &= \frac{\sum_{t} \text{pen}_t \cdot \mathbf{1}[\text{Bloom} \geq 4] \cdot w_t}{\sum_{t} w_t}
\end{align}

Independent indices.  An occupation can be high on both, low on both,
or any combination.  No diagonal constraint.

\medskip
\noindent\textit{Why not embeddings?}
Tried it.  Sentence-transformers on O*NET cluster by \emph{domain},
not by \emph{cognitive mode}.  The bureaucratic uniformity of the text
kills the semantic signal.  The verb carries the mode; the noun carries
the domain.  Bloom's extracts the former and discards the latter.

\section*{What This Opens Up}
%==================================================

\textbf{1.\ Horse race.}
Two indices in the same DiD.  Does automation predict displacement
while augmentation does not?  Or do they interact?

\medskip
\textbf{2.\ Sharpens every existing exposure measure.}
Eloundou, Felten, Webb all conflate substitution and complementarity
into one number.  This decomposes them --- without proprietary
interaction-type data.

\medskip
\textbf{3.\ Bridges theory and measurement.}
AutoIndex $\leftrightarrow$ $s(i)$ in task models: the supply shock
that displaces workers.  AugIndex $\leftrightarrow$ the augmentation
channel that every paper sets aside as ``future work.''

\section*{Open Questions}
%==================================================

\begin{itemize}
  \item Bloom level 3 (Apply) is the biggest bin and the hardest call.
        Sensitivity: move it to augmentation.  Results should be robust.
  \item Penetration is Claude-specific.  Cross-validate when GPT
        usage data becomes available.
  \item The verb--noun interaction: ``analyze \emph{data}'' vs.\
        ``analyze \emph{policy implications}'' may carry different
        automation potential.  The current method ignores this.
        Room for refinement.
  \item Empirical horse-race results are preliminary.  Reporting
        them here would be premature.
\end{itemize}
