** Plan: Constructing "Needs a Human" vs "Doesn't Need a Human" Indices **
** REVISED v3: 2D Bloom's Matrix (verb × knowledge depth) **

Background:
  v1 (embeddings + K-means) → clustered by domain, not cognitive mode. Dead.
  v2 (verb-only Bloom) → works, but ignores the knowledge dimension.
      "Analyze data" and "analyze policy implications" get the same score.
      That's wrong — the second one needs expertise, the first one doesn't.

Insight from Anderson & Krathwohl (2001) revised Bloom's:
  The taxonomy is TWO-DIMENSIONAL:
    X-axis: Cognitive Process (verb)  → Remember ... Create
    Y-axis: Knowledge Dimension (noun) → Factual ... Metacognitive

  The verb tells you WHAT the brain is doing.
  The noun tells you HOW DEEP the knowledge needs to be.
  AI substitutability depends on BOTH.

  Bottom-left (factual × remember) = no human needed.
  Top-right (metacognitive × create) = human essential.
  The middle is where the interesting variation is.

Pragmatic implementation:
  We already have the verb axis (Bloom levels 1-6 from spaCy).
  For the knowledge axis, use O*NET JOB ZONE as a proxy for expertise depth:
    Job Zone 1-2 (no degree / HS) ≈ factual / procedural knowledge
    Job Zone 3   (some college)    ≈ conceptual knowledge
    Job Zone 4-5 (BA / graduate)   ≈ conceptual / metacognitive knowledge

  This is essentially what "expertise" means in Autor's framework —
  how much domain-specific knowledge does the task require?

  Job Zone is available in the Anthropic HuggingFace repo (wage_data.csv)
  and in O*NET directly. It's at the occupation level — every task inherits
  its occupation's Job Zone.

Scoring: "Needs a human" matrix
  For each task, compute a 2D score:
    bloom_verb = Bloom level 1-6 (from spaCy, same as v2)
    job_zone   = 1-5 (from O*NET, occupation-level)

  "Needs a human" score = f(bloom_verb, job_zone)
  Simple version:
    human_score = (bloom_verb / 6) × 0.5 + (job_zone / 5) × 0.5
    → continuous 0-1 score, higher = more human-essential
    → automation_score = 1 - human_score

  Or use the matrix cutoff:
    "Doesn't need a human" if bloom ≤ 3 AND job_zone ≤ 2
    "Needs a human" if bloom ≥ 4 OR job_zone ≥ 4
    Middle zone: ambiguous, score proportionally

Data inputs:
  - raw/Anthropic Task/task_penetration.csv  (task × penetration)
  - raw/Crosswalks/onet_task_statements.csv  (task → occ crosswalk)
  - wage_data.csv from Anthropic HuggingFace (occ → JobZone)
    OR O*NET Job Zones file directly

Steps:
  1. Extract root verb (spaCy — already done)
  2. Classify verb into Bloom level (already done)
  3. NEW: Merge Job Zone onto each task via occupation code
  4. Compute 2D score: human_score = f(bloom, job_zone)
  5. Aggregate to occupation:
     AutoIndex_o  = Σ (pen_t × (1 - human_score_t) × w_t) / Σ w_t
     AssIndex_o   = Σ (pen_t × human_score_t × w_t) / Σ w_t
  6. Export, visualize, run regressions (same pipeline as before)

Key advantage over v2:
  "Calculate tax liabilities" (apply × procedural, JZ=3) gets a DIFFERENT
  score than "Evaluate treatment options" (evaluate × conceptual, JZ=4)
  even though both have moderate Bloom levels.

  The Job Zone dimension captures exactly the expertise that determines
  whether AI can do the task alone or needs a human in the loop.
