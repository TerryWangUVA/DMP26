\noindent\textit{Sample:} CPS-ASEC 2018--2025, labor-force participants aged 22--65 with at least a high school diploma ($N\approx 500{,}669$). AI exposure is the Eloundou et al.\ (2024) GPT-4 $\beta$ score (\texttt{dv\_rating\_beta}), ranging from 0 to $\approx$0.78, assigned at the occupation level and time-invariant. \textit{College} = BA or above (educ $>$ 110). \textit{Non-college} = high school or some college (educ $\leq$ 110).

\caption{Kernel density of AI exposure ($\beta_o$): college-educated workers (BA or above, navy solid) vs.\ non-college workers (high school or some college, maroon dashed).}
\label{fig:kdensity_educ}
\end{center}
\smallskip
\noindent\footnotesize\textit{Notes:} Each observation is a worker-year. The two distributions show the occupational exposure profile faced by each education group. If the distributions overlap substantially, the asymmetric wage result (non-college compressed, college not) is more striking because it cannot be attributed simply to differential exposure.
\end{figure}
